Orion

An aircraft asset tracking and management app

#Mobile APP

Project Brief

Orion is a mobile application that allows users to collect real-time data of their flight equipment during logistics transportation. The collected data is then sent to the main platform, helping customers better manage their aviation assets.

The Problem

To help customers manage their aviation assets, this project requires a mobile application to support data collection for the main platform. Only with accurate data can the main platform develop effective management plans to minimize customer losses.

Goal

  • Simple and easy to understand: Ensure users can easily grasp and use each feature
  • Adapt to the user's real work environment: Consider the user's needs and use scenarios in their actual work, ensuring the app matches their work environment
  • Maintain design consistency: Ensure consistency with the main platform's design, so users feel familiar and comfortable when seamlessly switching between the mobile app and the main platform

My Role

  • Provide support and guidance for the design through research and data collection with stakeholders and the development team
  • In the mobile app version, lead the design direction by optimizing and rebuilding the design system (e.g., fonts, colors, components)
  • Use Figma for wireframing and prototype design to ensure the accuracy and visual effectiveness of the design
  • Present the prototype to stakeholders to gather feedback and insights
  • Iterate and improve the prototype based on feedback to ensure the final design meets user needs and expectations

Step I

Learn and Be Curious at first

During the meeting with the stockholders, I asked and learned about the app's main purpose, including its functionality, target users, and some design style requirements. This step is to get a basic idea of my design direction and how to create a user-friendly but interesting and unique app around it.

Insight #1: Work environment

Orion is primarily intended for pilots as the primary user, and because their working hours may vary depending on flight distance, the day and night modes is a important feature

Insight #2: Consistent with the main platform

Orion Mobile is merely an add-on app to the main platform. The main platform will take over once the user has gathered the data on the app. As a result, maintaining consistency with the main platform's design will reduce user confusion.

Insight #3: Use of language

Incorporate some Pilot's terms in the app's design to enhance user-friendliness, but balance their use to avoid confusion for non-pilot users. Provide explanations and context to ensure all users can understand and navigate the app.

Step II

Wireframing

This time, I was able to get started on my wireframe idea quickly because the other team had already given me a project brief of what functions each page should have in the meeting. All I had to do was create and combine these "puzzles" as needed to come up with some interesting and useful interfaces.

Design system & Prototyping

Logo & Color Palette

The app's main color is navy, and some primary and secondary colors are employed to highlight some informative elements and reflect the theme of the app.

Typeface

The typeface used is a type of San-serif typeface that can highlight the sense of technology; it is not a common typeface used in app, but it matches the idea of the product better

Reusable Components

These reusable components are frequently used in the app's design, saving time. They are designed in both day and night modes, allowing for future improvements. Simplified with Figma's properties function, the components will be more flexible, smaller, and easier to manage as the project evolves.

Step III

Before & After

We received positive feedback from clients after showing and testing the demo. And we made the following changes to the prototype:

Improvement #1: Update unit formats and reorganize the list.

Modified the information and adjusted the layout to better meet user needs.

Improvement #2: Redesigned the timestamp.

The initial timestamp design was overly complex due to excessive details. A box-style design was adopted to improve readability.
