Jaundice Check

A real-time jaundice monitoring app connecting doctors and parents

#WeChat App

Project Overview

This app was developed for E3A Healthcare as a companion tool for their smart jaundice meter. It includes both hospital and patient interfaces. By combining hardware and software, it helps medical staff collect and manage newborn jaundice data in real time, while providing parents with clear visual feedback and health tips ‚Äî forming a closed-loop monitoring system

Problem Statement

Jaundice is a common condition in newborns, but traditional monitoring and communication methods are inefficient and error-prone. Medical staff often rely on manual or paper-based processes, and parents lack clear data feedback, leading to anxiety and communication issues that impact care quality.

Goal

  • Build a dual-end WeChat mini program that efficiently syncs with the device and visualizes health data
  • Doctor side: Optimize workflows for quick data entry, viewing, and management
  • Parent side: Help parents understand their baby‚Äôs condition through easy-to-read visual charts and timely care tips
  • Collaboration: Enable real-time data sync, alerts, and two-way communication to improve the doctor-parent experience

My Role

  • Designing both interfaces: Tailored to the different needs of hospitals and families
  • Building a design system: With reusable components and visual standards for consistency and scalability
  • Creating visual assets: Including icons, charts, and status indicators
  • User research & analysis: Collaborated with the marketing team to iterate based on user interviews and feedback
  • Design implementation: Worked closely with frontend and backend developers to balance usability, feasibility, and business goals

1.Understanding Users & Needs

Getting Inside the Users‚Äô World

Before starting the design phase, I worked closely with the marketing team, developers, and external collaborators to understand how the product would be used across roles.

Since the mini program mainly serves parents, I focused on their post-detection behavior: how they check results, whether they understand the numbers, and what features they need most. With help from user interviews and customer support feedback, I identified three key needs from the parent side: Quick access to results Easy understanding of medical data Guided next steps

Doctors, while not the main users, support remote Q&A and follow-ups. So, their interface also needed to be simple and clear for quick decision-making.

Insight #1: Quick Access

Users want to see key data right away, without navigating through layers. Many apps overwhelm with complex menus, making information harder to find

Insight #2: Easy Understanding

Parents, especially first-timers, often lack a medical background. Data must be visualized and explained to reduce anxiety and support informed decisions

Insight #3: Guided Experience

Different user roles need tailored flows: parents need results and advice, while doctors need fast access to records and Q&A. Good flow design improves clarity and efficiency.
