Voop

Professional automated business proposal generation software

#Software

Project Overview

Voop is a tool that helps companies automate proposal creation. It offers marketing teams and project managers a more efficient way to collaborate and organize proposals — saving time by removing the need to search for old content, rewrite similar sections, and adjust formatting manually.

Goal

Simplifies the proposal creation process with template selection, auto-filled content, and customizable design. It also supports role-based features and permissions tailored to different user types, such as marketers and business managers.

Paint Point

Efficiency Gaps

After meeting with marketing teams and project managers, I identified common pain points in proposal creation—repetitive content, formatting issues, and the challenge of customization. These insights inspired me to design Voop, aiming to improve efficiency and quality through templated and automated proposal generation.

Pain Point #1: Redundancy

Voop was inspired by a leadership meeting where our PM highlighted that proposal teams spend more time on formatting and compiling old materials than creating new content. Tasks like updating client names, adjusting layouts, and organizing structure consume most of their time.

Pain Point #2: Inconsistency

Documents from different teams vary wildly—some use Word, others messy PPTs, or even PDF screenshots. This lack of standardization harms the company’s professional image and affects how clients perceive us.

Pain Point #3: Confusion

Despite having templates and writing guides, marketing team members find them unclear and hard to follow. Without structured guidance, templates are often ignored, leading to inconsistent proposals and slower workflows.

It's brainstorming time

Starting Point

Based on the pain points mentioned above, I understood that users don’t struggle with writing, but with knowing where to start and how to structure their proposals. Therefore, my initial goal wasn’t to design a flashy template, but to create a proposal generation process that is intuitive, structured, and logically clear.

3.Build

Wireframe

Login -> Dashboard -> Create Proposal -> Export Flow

This section showcases the core workflow of Voop during the MVP phase. Starting from login, users proceed through the homepage, create a proposal, select a template, enter the editor, and finally export the proposal. Each step aligns with user needs and provides a clear, logical experience, laying the groundwork for future feature expansion.

① Users log in through the main interface； ② After logging in, they will be directed to the Dashboard to create a new proposal. *To modify an existing proposal, simply click on the target file in the upper section.

① Users select the questions needed for the proposal. The logic is that by selecting the required questions, the system automatically generates the overall outline, which can then be edited (using data from the company's database)；② Users input the basic information for the proposal；③ next step； ④ choose a preferred template.

*This is divided into two different scenarios: the green path is for higher-level users, and the orange path is for regular-level users. G① After editing, click the export button on the left to export; G② Select the options that meet the user's needs and then export； G③ Export is completed. O① Regular users click the export button on the left, triggering a modal to submit an export permission request; O② Once permissions are granted, the export button is enabled (during the waiting period, users can check the application status or the export progress through the toolbar on the left); O③ The remaining steps are the same as the green path.

Reflection

Voop is a proposal document generation tool for marketing teams and project managers, aimed at solving issues like inconsistent formatting, tedious content updates, and unclear collaboration. By focusing on user experience and conducting interviews and process analysis, we identified key needs such as template flexibility, content automation, and permission control. Through wireframing and testing, we improved workflows and team collaboration. This project strengthened my skills in functional design, team cooperation, and balancing complexity with user experience.
