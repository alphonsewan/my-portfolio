Parents' Note

A caring assistant during pregnancy

#Mobile App

Project Brief

The Parent's Note mobile app provides pregnant couples with a variety of helpful tools, such as daily pregnancy tips, dos and don'ts, community, daily logs, and more.

The Problem

Pregnancy can present a variety of new issues for couples. For instance, the wife might lose her memory or go through emotional changes as a result of the pregnancy, and the couple might be unsure of what to do at that time.

Goal

  • Provide an easy-to-use and comprehensive application to support couples with their needs and challenges during pregnancy
  • By providing professional pregnancy information, reminders, and guidance, help couples better understand and manage the physical changes and emotional fluctuations during pregnancy
  • Provide specific features, such as shared schedules, task assignments, and pregnancy progress tracking, to meet the personalized needs of couples
  • Design a cute and friendly brand image and design system that makes users feel warm, comfortable, and joyful, thereby enhancing user satisfaction and loyalty

My Role

  • Conduct user research to understand the needs, preferences, and pain points of the target audience
  • Analyze competitors and industry trends to identify opportunities for innovation and differentiation
  • Use wireframes to outline the project structure, and create interactive prototypes in Figma to simulate user interactions with the product
  • Conduct usability testing by inviting users to participate in the product testing process

Main Features Showcase

Helpful tool kits

The Upcoming tests and screenings feature aids expectant couples in remembering upcoming appointments, and the Hospital Bag feature helps in remembering insurance, hospital details, and the best route from the user's location to the hospital. As well as a Can / Can't feature to assist couples in determining what they can and cannot eat/do, etc.

Connection

The connection here refers to the data link between the couple's accounts, which allows the husband to be more aware of what his wife is recording and to assist her. For instance, if the wife has recorded her next doctor's visit, the husband's side will be notified as well, allowing him to become an additional reminder.

Real-Time Reminders

This feature provides a timely reminder for couples who have already connected their accounts. This video uses a scenario to show how the husband will be notified if his wife records a sad mood for the first time that day using the mood function.

Step I

Getting to know the users

To gain valuable insights, I used several research methods. First, I did in-depth interviews with pregnant couples at different stages. I listened to their needs and challenges. Next, I studied the interface design, features, and user experience of similar applications. I also reviewed relevant literature and industry reports. Through these methods, I gained comprehensive user insights and market intelligence. This provided reliable guidance for the design process.

Insight #1: The features that users truly require

After interviewing some pregnant couples and utilizing similar apps, I discovered that not all of the functionalities are actually required by the users. Some of the full-featured pregnancy apps are so complicated that users struggle to find the functions they need quickly. But some of them are too simplistic to suit their needs.

Insight #2: The difference between male and female users

Users who are men are more interested in learning about what they should be aware of during their wives' pregnancies and how they may better assist their spouses. Female users want to be able to communicate with other moms in addition to the basic features of recording and querying information.

Insight #3: Focusing on doing my own part

According to the interviews, several apps have added merchandising features to encourage users to buy mom and baby products, which includes advertisements. None of the three couples I interviewed were happy about it.

Step II

It's brainstorming time

Using the gathered information, I began constructing the application's sitemap and wireframes. This was primarily to define the structure of the app and the flow of user interactions. By creating the sitemap, I was able to clearly understand the relationships between the various modules of the app and ensure users could smoothly navigate and use the app's features. The wireframes were used to describe the page layout and element arrangement, helping me further refine the design and interaction details of the user interface.

Wireframe

The application is divided into three sections: the homepage, community page, and account management page. In this step, I began considering how to design the layout and other functional details to enhance the user experience. For instance, on the homepage toolbar, I included the ability for users to customize and adjust features based on their preferences.

Step III

Bring it all together

After completing the wireframes, I integrated the brand style and created a low-fidelity prototype. This stage focused on building an interactive interface to test and validate the design. I added basic features and navigation, keeping the design simple and user-friendly while ensuring consistent branding. User feedback will help optimize the design for the final high-fidelity prototype, ensuring a great user experience.

Step IV

Parents' Note begins to meet"friends"

The testing involved couples at different stages: early pregnancy, late pregnancy, and those with previous childbirth experience. The main testing tasks included account registration, testing the mood tracking feature, and using all the small tools they would typically need.

Improvement #1: Reduce the number of steps in the functionality

The mood tracking feature was adjusted by replacing the previous multi-step process with a simpler approach, where users complete it through a pop-up on their first login each day.

Improvement #2: Optimize the appointment scheduling feature

The visibility of each test item was increased, and a record-keeping feature was added. Additionally, more appointment information and record features were included within each test item.

Improvement #3: Optimize the navigation bar.

The fixed navigation bar was changed to a collapsible format, providing more reading space for users.

Step V

Demo View

Reflection

I focused on user experience, continuously collecting feedback and making optimizations. Through user research, competitive analysis, and user testing, I integrated these insights into the design. This approach allows users to easily find the features they need while offering a personalized experience to meet diverse needs. By interacting with users and gathering feedback in the later stages, I consistently refined the design to ensure the best possible experience. Throughout the design process, I paid attention to detail and the emotional expression of the user, believing that this app will help expectant couples better enjoy their pregnancy journey and access the information and support they need.
